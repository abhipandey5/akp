Research Interests

My primary research interests are in the fields of cybersecurity, machine learning, and healthcare data security. I am deeply engaged in developing innovative solutions to enhance the privacy and security of sensitive healthcare information, with a special focus on:

  • Cryptography and secure communication protocols
  • Machine Learning applications in cybersecurity
  • Authentication Systems and secure user verification
  • Cyber-Physical Systems and their security challenges
  • Health Informatics for data management and security
  • Malware Analysis for identifying and mitigating threats

Current Research Projects

Currently, I am involved in a number of exciting projects that explore innovative approaches to enhancing cybersecurity and data protection:

Machine Learning-Based Authentication for Healthcare Systems

This project focuses on developing advanced authentication techniques using machine learning to secure sensitive healthcare data. The goal is to create robust and adaptive security systems that can protect against emerging threats while maintaining ease of use for healthcare professionals.

Cybersecurity in Cyber-Physical Systems

We are exploring security challenges in Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS), particularly in sectors like healthcare and transportation, to ensure the integrity and confidentiality of physical devices interacting with digital systems.
